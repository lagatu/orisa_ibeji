Calculation Group Spike – Execution Plan

Step 1 — Clarify the Expected Deliverable

Ask your manager or mentor:

- For the Calculation Group spike, would you like a recommendation document only, or would you like me to create the follow-up Jira stories as part of the spike?
- Is there any previous analysis, documentation, or architecture decision record regarding Calculation Groups that I should review before starting?
- Are there specific business reports or dashboards that are driving this evaluation?

Step 2 — Gather Context

Understand why the spike was created.

Questions to answer:
- What problem are we trying to solve?
- Is there a performance issue?
- Is there a maintainability issue?
- Is there confusion among report developers?
- Is this related to the Databricks/Fabric migration?
- Did someone recommend removing Calculation Groups?

Document answers.

Step 3 — Review the Existing Semantic Model

In Visual Studio review:
- Fact Tables
- Dimensions
- Measures
- Calculation Groups
- Perspectives
- Display Folders

Create notes such as:
Calculation Group Exists: Yes/No
Name:
Calculation Items:

Fact Tables:
- Fact_AUM
- Fact_Client
- Fact_Account

Step 4 — Review Existing Reports

Look at:
- Power BI reports
- Dashboards
- Common KPIs

Identify:
- Total AUM
- AUM Accounts
- AUM Clients
- Marketing Campaigns
- Lead Conversion

Ask:
Are users dragging columns directly into visuals, or are they using measures?

Step 5 — Review Measure Design

For each fact table, count:
- Number of Measures
- Number of Numeric Columns

Example:

Fact_AUM

Columns:
- AUMValue
- AccountCount
- ClientCount

Measures:
- Total AUM
- Total Accounts

Observation:
ClientCount has no explicit measure.

Step 6 — Identify Implicit Aggregation Usage

Look for situations where users rely on:
- SUM(Column)
- COUNT(Column)
- AVG(Column)

instead of:
- [Total AUM]
- [Total Clients]
- [Total Accounts]

Create findings.

Step 7 — Review Calculation Group Usage

If a Time Intelligence group exists, document:
- Current
- MTD
- QTD
- YTD
- Prior Year
- YoY
- YoY %

Ask:
Which reports currently use these calculations?

Step 8 — Analyze Pros and Cons

Keep Calculation Groups:
- Centralized DAX
- Easier maintenance
- Consistent time intelligence
- Reusable logic

Remove Calculation Groups:
- More duplicated DAX
- Simpler for some report authors
- Less governance
- Higher maintenance effort

Step 9 — Form Recommendation

Most likely outcome:
- Keep Calculation Groups
- Create Explicit Measures
- Avoid Implicit Aggregations

Reason:
Calculation Groups require explicit measures and provide better governance and consistency.

Step 10 — Create Jira Stories

Story 1:
Re-implement Time Intelligence Calculation Group

Story 2:
Create Explicit Measures for Marketing Fact Tables

Story 3:
Validate Time Intelligence Calculations with Business Users

Step 11 — Prepare Spike Deliverable

Structure:
1. Objective
2. Current State
3. Findings
4. Analysis
5. Recommendation
6. Proposed Stories
7. Risks and Considerations

Step 12 — Present Like a Senior Engineer

Suggested summary:

I reviewed the semantic model and current reporting patterns.

The primary finding is that Calculation Groups provide reusable and governed time intelligence logic, but their value is realized only when reports leverage explicit measures.

My recommendation is to retain Calculation Groups and standardize explicit aggregation measures across the Marketing fact tables.
